East Blue Saga


Romance Dawn Arc

The Golden Age of Piracy has gone into full bloom due to the pirate Gol D. Roger finding the One Piece, the greatest treasure known to man. Despite his execution by the navy, his dying words instructing people the world over to find his greatest treasure.

Meanwhile, in a quaint little village, Monkey D. Luffy is born. He’s accidentally eaten a devil fruit, mystical fruits that grant magical abilities at the cost of your ability to swim. Luffy’s fruit, the Gum Gum fruit, allows him to stretch like rubber. His idol is the pirate Red-Haired Shanks, who saves his life from a sea serpent, sacrificing his arm for his friend. Before Shanks leaves, he gifts Luffy his Straw Hat, which becomes Luffy’s most prized possession.Luffy is so inspired by Shanks he decides once he is old enough, he will set sail and find the One Piece and become the new King of the Pirates.

After rescuing timid aspiring marine Koby from the pirate Alvida, Luffy moves on to finding the crew he needs to take on the world. His first crewmate is the swordsman Roronoa Zoro who uses the 3-sword style. He recruits Zoro after they take down a corrupt marine base and Koby joins the marines after inspiration and words of encouragement from Luffy.

Orange Town Arc

Zoro and Luffy move on to the next island where they encounter amateur thief Nami who is currently robbing the clown pirate Buggy who is terrorizing the local town. We find out that Buggy apprenticed on the same pirate ship as Shanks and Luffy officially beats him. Nami however, claims to hate pirates and despite Luffy’s requests, only agrees to a temporary alliance and not a permanent crew spot.

Syrup Village Arc

After Nami forms a partnership with the two as their navigator, the crew reaches Syrup Village and searches for a ship to use. There, they encounter a chronic liar named Usopp. When a devious plot is revealed, Usopp's courage is put to the test as the crew prepare for a battle with the Black Cat. They recruit their sniper Usopp, who wants to be a brave pirate warrior, just like his dad Yasopp who is a member of Shank’s crew. Usopp’s childhood friend, Kaya also gives Luffy and crew their first ship, the Going Merry. Pirates.

Baratie Arc

In search of a cook for their ship, the crew go to a sea restaurant known as the Baratie. However, the restaurant is attacked by the powerful Don Krieg, who has returned from the Grand Line after being attacked by Dracule Mihawk, and wants to use the Baratie as a vessel to go back.After drifting for a while, Luffy encounters the floating resturant Baratie where they encounter gentleman chef Sanji, who Luffy wants to join the crew. After defeating the pirate Don Krieg attacking Baratie, Sanji finally joins the crew.

Arlong Park Arc

Sanji from the Baratie joining the crew and Nami stealing the ship, the crew follow her to an island under the control of the fish-man pirate Arlong. Nami reveals that she is a member of the Arlong Pirates and simply helped the crew so she could take their gold later. But why would she follow a man like Arlong? We learn that Nami is essentially a slave to Arlong, an evil fishman a race of aquatic humanoids who resemble marine animals. Luffy defeats Arlong and Nami truly gains respect and admiration for him, officially joining the crew as navigator.

Loguetown Arc

After Nami officially joins,Through Nami we learn the ultimate goal of Luffy’s voyage and his best shot at locating the One Piece is to sail through the Grand Line, a strip of islands that divides the ‘One Piece’ planet into it’s two hemispheres. It’s where the most powerful pirates lie and where the rest of the story will take place.

Alabasta Island Arc


Reverse Mountain Arc

The crew has entered the Grand Line, but they encounter an obstacle as soon as they get in, a giant whale that swallows the entire ship.And they find out that the whale is waiting for there old human friends return,The pirates friends promised that they would come back for Laboon someday, so the whale still awaits their return to this day,Also inside the whale are two mysterious rogues.

Whisky Peak Arc

The Straw Hats have their Log Pose (Grand Line compass) and are heading for an island that welcomes pirates with open arms, but dark secrets are also at play. With that triumph under their belt, they are directed to the town of Whiskey Peak which is reported to openly welcome pirates and treat them like celebrities. On the island, the Straw Hats party hard under a false sense of security. It isn’t until the main crew is asleep that it’s revealed that all the residents of Whiskey Peak are secretly assassins and bounty hunters. In fact, the town turns out to be a part of the same group that Mr. 9 and Miss Wednesday are from, the two bounty hunters who were introduced in the Reverse Mountain arc. However, Zoro is aware of the town’s true intentions, faking his drunken stupor and sobering up to fight almost every bounty hunter in Whiskey Peak. Luffy’s misunderstanding that Zoro was hurting the residents of Whiskey Peak for no apparent reason. It’s a hilarious fight before they eventually both understand and team up against the Whiskey Peak residents. The most important development is the revelation that Miss Wednesday is, in fact, Princess Nefertari Vivi, who went undercover in the organization that we now know is called Baroque Works.

Little Garden Arc

On their mission to bring Princess Vivi, who was undercover in Baroque Works as Ms. Wednesday, to Arabasta, the crew land at Little Garden, which is actually a huge island that is still stuck in the prehistoric era. However, there are more than dinosaurs on this island, as the crew soon finds out when they encounter two giants that have been locked in combat for over 100 years. However, their friendly interactions are cut short when Baroque Works interrupts their duel, earning the ire of Dorry, Broggy, and the entire Straw Hat crew. The Little Garden story arc also established new stakes for the Straw Hat Pirates crew, with Nami catching a dreadful illness that no one could cure. So, the Straw Hats had to venture to Drum Island and seek a doctor.

Drum Island Arc

To cure Nami the Straw Hats need to make a little detour to the kingless land, Drum Island. Before getting there, they have a little run-in with the pirate Wapol, who is the wielder of the Baku Baku no Mi Devil Fruit, allowing him to eat anything they please and manifest it within his body. Luffy deals with him with no problem, and he and his crew head onward. To get to Dr. Kureha, Sanji and Luffy must carry Nami on a treacherous path up the highest mountain to Drum Castle. After being attacked on the way, Luffy winds up carrying Sanji and Nami, injuring himself in the process. Luckily, they manage to make it to the castle just as Luffy collapses. Meanwhile, Wapol returns to his former kingdom and overpowers the militia before heading to his old home, Drum Castle. With Wapol defeated and Nami better, Luffy convinces Chopper to join his crew—after all, a medic would be very handy! Chopper agrees to go and the island gives them a great send-off allowing the Straw Hats to continue on their journey to deliver Princess Vivi to Alabasta.

Albasta Arc

When the Straw Hats reach the kingdom of Alabasta, we learn that Baroque Works is ruling it from the shadows. We learn that Crocodile, the leader of Baroque Works, is one of the Seven Warlords of the Sea, a group of extremely strong pirates that have actually made deals with the navy.Before the civil war starts Vivi must reach the rebels and tell them the real cause behind the conflict, but Crocodile the leader of Baroque Works and one of the Seven Warlords of the Sea, will not just stand by and watch. In the end, Baroque Works, marine forces, pirates, the royal guards, and the rebel army clash in a battle that will determine the fate of the country itself. Nami obtains her Clima-Tact; Roronoa Zoro receives his first wanted poster; Luffy saves Nico Robin's life and Crocodile's Warlord title is revoked.
